Hidden hunger is a form of malnutrition. It refers to nutrition gaps in your diet even when you're full and/or get enough calories. Specifically, it refers to a lack of essential nutrients, including micronutrients like vitamin A or C, that are crucial to your health.

Hidden Hunger can lead to various health problems, including reduced immune response, impaired physical development, cognitive issues, increased risk of chronic diseases, and more.

It affects 2 billion people worldwide, including otherwise healthy people and millions here in America.

There are many symptoms of Hidden Hunger, but they can be difficult to recognize because they can be subtle and may not be obvious.

However, they include unexplained fatigue, increased bouts of sickness, impaired growth, mood changes, poor concentration, thinking problems, appetite changes, and more.
